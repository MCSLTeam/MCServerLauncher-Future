# Code of Conduct

## Communication

- Keep discussion respectful, technical, and focused on the work.
- Review code with evidence: cite behavior, tests, docs, or concrete risks.
- State uncertainty directly. Do not hide assumptions or unresolved risk.

## Collaboration

- Do not revert, overwrite, or discard someone else's work without explicit instruction.
- Separate personal preference from project rules.
- Prefer small, reviewable changes with clear verification.

## Privacy And Safety

- Do not commit secrets, private tokens, personal data, signing keys, or local credentials.
- Do not publish private contact information without explicit permission.
- Report security-sensitive issues through the project maintainers instead of public disclosure.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions that violate these standards. Serious or repeated violations may lead to loss of participation privileges.
